Why Empathy Defines Great Leaders

Published Oct 16, 2025 · Leadership

In a quiet village, there were two potters. Both crafted beautiful pots, but only one had a long line of customers. When asked why, he said, “I listen to what they need. Some want a pot to hold rice, some want it for flowers, others for water. Clay bends easily when you understand its purpose.”

Leadership is a lot like pottery. People respond to how they are treated. Too much pressure and they crack; too little and they lose shape. Empathy helps a leader find the right balance.

“Leadership is not about being in charge. It is about taking care of those in your charge.”
— Simon Sinek

What Empathy Looks Like at Work

  • Listening beyond status updates: noticing silence and drawing out concerns.
  • Designing for constraints: workload, time zones, and energy levels matter.
  • Making decisions visible: explain the “why” and close the loop.

Practices You Can Start Today

  1. Open with purpose: state the outcome and who it helps.
  2. Invite dissent: ask, “What might we be missing?”
  3. Share credit, own gaps: celebrate the team; take responsibility for misses.
